About the Lawsuit

Talcum Powder Has Been Linked to Ovarian Cancer

Decades of scientific research have established a link between regular use of talcum powder in the genital area and the development of ovarian cancer. Major manufacturers knew about these risks but failed to warn consumers.

The Medical Evidence

Multiple peer-reviewed studies have shown that women who regularly applied talcum powder to the perineal area face an increased risk of developing ovarian cancer. Talc particles can travel through the reproductive tract and cause chronic inflammation leading to cancer.

The Legal Action

Juries have awarded billions of dollars to women harmed by talcum powder products. Lawsuits allege manufacturers like Johnson & Johnson knew for decades that their talc products were contaminated and posed cancer risks, yet continued to sell them without adequate warnings.

Eligibility

Do You Qualify?

You may be eligible if the following apply to you:

Diagnosed with Qualifying Ovarian Cancer

You have been diagnosed with epithelial ovarian carcinoma, clear cell carcinoma, serous carcinoma, fallopian tube cancer, primary peritoneal carcinoma, or another qualifying ovarian cancer type.

Diagnosis Within the Last 10 Years

Your cancer diagnosis occurred between 2016 and the present day, and you were between ages 18 and 70 at the time of diagnosis.

Used Talcum Powder for 4+ Years

You regularly used talcum powder products (such as baby powder) in the perineal/genital area for at least 4 years before your diagnosis.
